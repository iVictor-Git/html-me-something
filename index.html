My Journey

I started coding back in high school around 2011 when I was in this basic computer class, I can't even remember the name of it, but I think it was Intro to Photoshop. While it was a class for Photoshop, the teacher gave us an assignment, to pair up with another in class and make a website.

It was at that time, that I was took my first crack at coding, predominately HTML5 and some CSS3. I was a total newbie. We had actually decided to do a movie review site. My responsibility was creating the pages and my partner was in charge of providing the content. Wasn't the best site, but hey, first attempt at coding so I got some experience with it.

Well, after that class, I ended up not really pursuing coding, until I entered college in 2014 (took a hiatus from school in 2013). It was in that year that I took a class in Python, called Intro to Python. The book assigned for reading taught me some basics, like different data types, variables, functions, etc, but there was so much information that it was overloading me. I was also taking Intro to Java at the same time, so I was basically learning 2 languages at the same time (probably shouldn't have done that). In any event, these were taught at the college (SPC), and I felt like I still didn't learn much.

Unsatisfied, I actually looked for resources online, and found Codecademy. It cemented some principles that I couldn't quite get and I had a better understanding of Python. I even learned OOP as well, but there were some concepts that eluded me. I had stopped learning Python after that, this was in around 2015. Thanks in part to LaunchCode 2017, I'm pretty comfortable in Python, and the concepts that were giving me trouble, dictionaries and tuples, are no longer vague concepts.

I don't intend to just know HTML5, CSS3, and Python. I plan to learn it all and become a Full-stack developer, I eventually will go into AI because I want to one day make a home system, run by AI. That's as far as I'm willing to share regarding that.
